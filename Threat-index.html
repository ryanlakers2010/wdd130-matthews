Threat Level Index

Before buying protection tools, it helps to know what you are actually defending against. Use our plain-English dictionary index to master core security terminology and concepts.

Phishing Attacks (Medium Risk)
Fake emails, messages, or websites engineered to look like genuine businesses (such as your bank) designed to trick you into typing in your passwords or credit card numbers.
Ransomware (High Risk)
A specialized type of malicious software that hijacks, locks, and encrypts your private files, holding them hostage until you pay the cybercriminal a specific fee.
Multi-Factor Authentication / MFA
A defense mechanism requiring two or more separate verification factors to gain system access (like a password combined with a text code), creating a crucial extra layer of security.
Virtual Private Networks / VPNs
A service that establishes an encrypted data tunnel for your device, completely masking your internet behavior and location from prying eyes on public networks.

Visualizing Security

Think of an unencrypted internet connection like a clear postcard anyone can read in transit. A VPN wraps that postcard in a heavy-duty titanium locked box.

Why MFA Matters

Even if a scammer steals your password through phishing, Multi-Factor Authentication stops them dead in their tracks because they don't have physical access to your device.
